About Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its stunning ring system, composed mostly of ice and rock particles. Saturn is a gas giant with a diameter nine times that of Earth and has more than 80 moons, including the largest moon, Titan.

Key Features

The Rings of Saturn

Saturn's iconic rings are made of ice and rock particles, and they are the most prominent and complex ring system of any planet in the Solar System.

Titan: Saturn's Largest Moon

Titan, Saturn's largest moon, is the only moon in the Solar System with a thick atmosphere and liquid lakes made of methane and ethane.

Storms on Saturn

Saturn experiences powerful storms, including the Great White Spot, a massive storm that appears every 30 Earth years.
