About Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our Solar System. Known for its striking blue color due to methane in its atmosphere, Neptune is a gas giant with intense winds and mysterious storm systems. It is the most distant planet that can be seen with the naked eye.

Key Features

The Great Dark Spot

The Great Dark Spot is a massive storm in Neptune's southern hemisphere, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

Fast Winds

Neptune has the fastest winds in the Solar System, with speeds reaching over 1,500 miles per hour.

Triton: Neptune's Largest Moon

Triton orbits Neptune in the opposite direction of the planet's rotation and is one of the few geologically active moons in the Solar System.
